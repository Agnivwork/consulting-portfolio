Proposal for Psychotherapy on Future Socioeconomic Well-being in Patients with Major Depressive Disorders in West Bengal

Objective: To evaluate the impact of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and antidepressants on the economic productivity and overall well-being of patients with Major Depressive Disorders (MDD) in West Bengal.

Methodology: Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT) with pre- and post-intervention surveys, economic productivity metrics, and psychological assessments. Statistical analysis was conducted using Stata and Python.

Key Findings: CBT and antidepressants significantly improved economic productivity among participants. The intervention group showed a 30% increase in work output compared to the control group.

Conclusion: The study demonstrated that integrating psychotherapy and medication can significantly enhance the socioeconomic well-being of individuals with MDD.

Investigating the Influence of India's Midday Meal Scheme on Primary Education and Nutrition

Objective: To assess the impact of the Midday Meal Scheme (MDM) on primary school enrollment, attendance, and nutritional status of children.

Methodology: Time-series data analysis from 1960 to 2014, using enrollment rates, Gross Enrollment Ratio (GER), and dropout rates. Regression analysis and Difference-in-Differences (DiD) approach were implemented using Stata.

Key Findings: The MDM scheme led to a significant increase in primary school enrollment, particularly among girls. Dropout rates decreased by 20% in the post-MDM period.

Conclusion: The MDM scheme has been successful in improving educational outcomes and nutritional status among primary school children.

Lessons from the Economic Collapse in Sri Lanka

Objective: To analyze the causes, consequences, and lessons of Sri Lanka's economic collapse, with a focus on policy failures, foreign debt, and external shocks.

Methodology: Macroeconomic trend analysis using data from the World Bank and Central Bank of Sri Lanka. Forecasting models were developed to identify early warning signs of economic crises.

Key Findings: Sri Lanka's economic collapse was primarily due to policy failures, excessive foreign debt, and the collapse of the tourism industry. The crisis led to severe shortages of food, medicine, and fuel.

Conclusion: The study highlights the importance of prudent fiscal policies and diversification of revenue sources to avoid similar economic pitfalls.

Effect of Change in Oil Prices and Its Effect on Consumption of Renewable Energy

Objective: To analyze the relationship between crude oil price fluctuations and the consumption of renewable energy.

Methodology: Statistical and econometric analysis using historical oil price data, macroeconomic indicators, and renewable energy consumption data from 2000 to 2020.

Key Findings: There is a significant negative correlation between crude oil prices and renewable energy consumption. Oil price volatility impacts macroeconomic stability, influencing investment in renewable energy.

Conclusion: The study underscores the importance of stable oil prices for promoting renewable energy adoption.

Investigating the Influence of India's Midday Meal Scheme on Primary Education and Nutrition (Extended Analysis)

Objective: To analyze gender-specific effects of the Midday Meal Scheme (MDM) on enrollment and dropout rates, and evaluate long-term trends in educational outcomes and nutritional status.

Methodology: Gender-disaggregated time-series analysis using data from 1960 to 2014. Regression analysis and Difference-in-Differences (DiD) approach were implemented using Stata.

Key Findings: The MDM scheme had a more significant impact on girls' enrollment and retention rates compared to boys. Long-term trends show sustained improvements in educational outcomes, particularly for girls.

Conclusion: The extended analysis highlights the importance of gender-sensitive policies in maximizing the impact of the MDM scheme.

Conclusion: The extended analysis underscores the need for proactive measures to build economic resilience in the face of external shocks.
